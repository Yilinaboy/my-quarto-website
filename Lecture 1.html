关键文章2

Cross-Border Flows of Students for Higher Education: Push–Pull Factors and Motivations of Mainland Chinese Students in Hong Kong and Macau (Li and Bray 2007)

Push and Pull Theory

  • Push Factors: These are the negative conditions in a student’s home country that drive them to seek education abroad. In the context of mainland Chinese students, push factors might include unfavorable educational conditions, limited opportunities for higher education, or socio-economic challenges in their home country.

  • Pull Factors: These are the attractive features of a host country that draw students to study there. For mainland Chinese students considering Hong Kong and Macau, pull factors could include advanced research facilities, scholarships, a conducive socio-economic and political environment, and the opportunity to study with multinational classmates.

  • Reverse Push-Pull Factors: The study introduces the concept of reverse push-pull factors, which include positive forces at home that encourage students to stay, such as the desire to remain with family, the relevance of domestic education, and the increasing internationalization of local institutions. Conversely, negative forces abroad, such as high costs, restrictive policies, and discrimination, can deter students from studying overseas.

  • Two-Way Model: The paper refines the traditional one-way push-pull model into a two-way model, acknowledging that both home and host countries have push and pull factors. This model considers not only the forces that drive students abroad but also those that encourage them to stay at home.

  • Personal Characteristics: The study highlights that the impact of push and pull factors is also influenced by personal characteristics such as socio-economic status, academic ability, motivation, and aspirations. These characteristics determine how different students respond to similar push and pull forces.

  • Dynamic Interplay: The decisions of students are shaped by the dynamic interplay between push and pull factors at home and abroad, as well as their personal perceptions and motivations. This interplay is crucial in understanding the mobility patterns of students.

Reference

Li, Mei, and Mark Bray. 2007. “Cross-Border Flows of Students for Higher Education: Push–Pull Factors and Motivations of Mainland Chinese Students in Hong Kong and Macau.” Higher Education 53 (6): 791–818. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10734-005-5423-3.
Mok, Ka Ho, Weiyan Xiong, Guoguo Ke, and Joyce Oi Wun Cheung. 2021. “Impact of COVID-19 Pandemic on International Higher Education and Student Mobility: Student Perspectives from Mainland China and Hong Kong.” International Journal of Educational Research 105: 101718. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijer.2020.101718.
